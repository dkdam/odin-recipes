Korean Fried Chicken


Home

Simple fried chicken thats normally made in korea and always best served with beer.

Ingredients

  • Boneless chicken(thigh) - 500-600 grams
  • Frying chicken mix powder(Korean brand if possible) - 1.5 cups
  • Cold water - 1.5 cups
  • Salt - 1 tsp
  • Black pepper
  • Minced Garlic - 1/2 tsp
  • Vinegar(Any type of white wine vinegar)

Prep The Chicken

  1. Get your chicken and frying chicken powder list.
  2. Cut the chicken to bite size pieces and put them in a bowl.
  3. Add 1tsp of salt, black pepper and 1/2tsp of minced garlic.
  4. Put 1tsp of vinegar and massage the chicken gently.
  5. Microwave the chicken for 30-60 seconds (Careful to not cook the surface of the chicken.)

Batter The Chicken

  1. Get a bowl then mix both the frying chicken powder & water in the ratio of 1:1 which you now have a batter.
  2. Now get the chicken and start dipping them into the batter one by one.
  3. Bring out a wider container and pour some frying powder evenly into it then put the dipped chicken on top of it.
  4. Pour some more powder on top of the dipped chicken, close the container lid and shake it like your life depends on it.

Fry The Chicken

  1. Prep the oil on the wok to a high temperature. To test when the best time to put the chicken in, take of a piece of batter from the container and put it into the oil. If it floats after 3 second it's ready.
  2. Now place all the chickens into the oil and let it cook til golden brown (est 10-12 minutes) then take it out.
  3. Double fry the chicken again on medium heat (est 8 minutes). This is make sure the chicken is properly cooked and crispy.
  4. Just check one of the chicken by cutting it to make sure its cooked inside.

That thats all done and enjoy your meal.
